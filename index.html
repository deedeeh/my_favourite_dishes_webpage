Egyptian

Koshari

Koshari is an Egyptian-street dish and it is one of my favourites on the Egyptian list. It is popular across Egypt as its pyramids are across the world. It consists of rice, lentils and pasta topped with tomato sauce and fried onions.

Stuffed pigeon

Stuffed pigeon is a very popular main dish in Egypt and many other countries in the Middle East. This dish consists of whole pigeons stuffed with rice. Whenever this plate is on the table beside so many other choices, I always pick stuffed pigeons.

Molokhia

Molokhia is a very healthy dish made of minced Jute leaves. This common dish in Egypt has three times the calcium and phospherous as Kale, and four times the amount of riboflavin. It also provides 70% of the RDA value for Vitamin C, 25% of the RDA of Vitamin A amongst a host of other minerals and vitamins.

Italian

Pizza Margherita

When Queen Margherita came to visit Naples in 1889, she was charmed by a local pizza baker who had made, in her honor, a pizza with the colors of the new flag of the just-unified Italy—red tomatoes, white mozzarella, and green basil. It’s now called the pizza margherita. The authentic italian pizza is an amazing choice when I am in a bad mood or I want to treat myself. I can eat pizza for 2-3 days and that is how much I love it!

Macaroni and cheese

There can be no doubt that its ultimate origins are Italian, as one finds macaroni and cheese recipes from the late thirteenth century in southern Italy. This is an easy dish to prepare and it could be done in a short time. My heart melts when the macaroni and cheese is creamy and full of cheese.

Spinach and ricotta cannelloni

Cannelloni are pasta tubes that have different fillings but my favourite and one of the most popular italian ones is the creamy spinach and ricotta filling, baked in a super simple and delicious tomato sauce and topped with melting mozzarella cheese. I prefer this filling over the minced beef and other options. I also like to add béchamel to it which will give it a creamier texture and taste.

Indian

Chicken tikka masala

Chicken tikka masala is a dish of chunks of roasted marinated chicken in a spiced curry. The curry is usually creamy and orange-coloured. I really love indian spices and sauces. The idea of loving indian food doesn't mean I love spicy food though. I prefer my tikka masala to be mild or medium.

Butter chicken

Butter chicken originated from Northern India in 1948. It is one of the most popular curries at any Indian restaurant around the world. It is prepared in a buttery gravy with the addition of cream gives the curry sauce a silky smooth rich texture. I tried it in few restaurants and unfortunately not many places can do it properly.

Naan bread

Naan is a leavened, oven-baked flatbread found in the cuisines mainly of Western Asia, South Asia, Indonesia, Myanmar and the Caribbean. It is made from maida, an Indian white flour that is very delicate and powdery, similar to pastry flour. It is eaten most often with curry, used instead of a spoon to sop up all of the sauce. I love to dip naan in indian sauces or even any sauce.

Asian

Chicken katsu curry

Chicken katsu curry is known as a western style dish in Japan. It compromises of panko breadcrumb coated chicken in a thick curry sauce and rice. First time I tried it was at Wasabi, a fast food rastaurant chain based in the UK, and I absolutely loved it. When I am close to Wasabi and I am feeling hungry I always choose katsu curry cause it is hard to resist a very tasty dish like that!
